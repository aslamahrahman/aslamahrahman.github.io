Work Experience

General Electric Global Research, Banglore
Structural Materials & Machine Learning Intern, Summer 2018

Nicker superalloys are some of the most complex alloy systems since they exhibit several anomalous characteristics such as an increase in strength when heated. While the anomalous behaviour makes presents challenges to their modeling studies, their application where such high strengths of the order of several 100s to 1000s MPa is required, such as in airplane engine turbines, is indispensable. Ther area of application is also hence critical to the margin of engineering error permissible, making it all the more important ot have accurate models to capture their behaviour. This makes it highly desirable to create models correlating composition-processing-properties using Machine Learning techniques and to capture all the hidden non-linearities and phase properties.

As an intern, I worked on preparing a proof of concept for using Random Forest Models for predicting properties of Nickel superalloys, which has the capacity of streamlining experimentation and diminishing R&D times by nearly 90%. I also investigated and assembled an expansive Nickel superalloys dataset covering nearly 90% of the alloy-space in terms of compositions from more than 200 publications. My models were capable of achieveing an error margin of ~5%, whereas the accepted industry standard is ~10%.
