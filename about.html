About Me

My journey in STEM started at the Indian Institute of Technology Madras, India, where I pursued a Bachelor of Technology degree in Metallurgical and Materials Engineering with a minor in Industrial Engineering. Being heavily influenced by my father who is a doctor and my elder sister who is a Computer Science major, I found myself gravitating towards opportunities where I could combine my interests and skills I had amassed through the courses and projects I had undertaken. In addition, my motivating principle in life is to make everything I pursue to be of some real help to people (to put it in very simplistic terms) that I was deeply drawn to how engineering has made inroads into medicine and biotechnology. Throughout my undergraduate degree, I pursued opportunities in this field, exploring computational biomaterials design and fluid transport in biomedical systems. I graduated with a thesis on Simulation and Modeling of Kinetic Evolution of Polymer Droplets during Electrospraying, which is an efficient way to manufacture capsules for controlled drug delivery internally.
